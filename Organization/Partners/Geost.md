## Summary

Geost (now part of Rocket Lab‚Äôs Optical Systems portfolio) is a U.S. developer of space-qualified electro-optical (EO) and infrared (IR) payloads used for missile warning/tracking, space domain awareness (SDA), and ISR missions. Rocket Lab markets Geost-derived payload families such as Heimdall (SDA), Phoenix (EO/ISR), and Starlite (IR) as modular sensing options for national security space architectures. Geost‚Äôs earlier product line included Mercury for missile warning/tracking; the technology and heritage behind these sensors has been selected for SDA Tracking Layer work (e.g., via Sierra Space for Tranche 2). Rocket Lab completed its acquisition of Geost on August 12, 2025, adding in-house EO/IR payload capability alongside launch and spacecraft manufacturing to support vertically integrated national security missions.

## Acquisition

Rocket Lab **announced** it had entered into a definitive agreement to acquire the **parent holding company of Geost, LLC** on **May 27, 2025**, buying the business from [**LightRidge Solutions**](https://www.lightridgesolutions.com/) (a portfolio company of **ATL Partners**). The transaction **closed on August 12, 2025**, formally adding **Optical Systems** (EO/IR payloads) to Rocket Lab‚Äôs space-systems portfolio. 

### Timeline 
* **May 27, 2025** - Deal announced (agreement signed May 22, 2025).
* **August 12, 2025** - Deal closed. 

### Deal terms (consideration + earnout)
* **Base purchase price:** **$275M** (before customary closing adjustments). 
* **Paid at closing:** **$125M cash** + **3,057,588 shares** of Rocket Lab common stock. 
* **Contingent earnout:** up to **$50M** in additional **cash** tied to revenue targets (described as revenue targets for **2026 and 2027** in the purchase agreement summary).
* **Equity mechanics:** the stock portion was described as **privately placed** / issued in an unregistered transaction, with Rocket Lab agreeing to file a **resale registration statement** for the shares after closing.
