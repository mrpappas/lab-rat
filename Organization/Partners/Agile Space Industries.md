### M4 Monopropellant Thruster

#### Summary 
M4 is a 4 N (1 lbf) hydrazine monopropellant thruster designed for attitude control and precision maneuvering of small spacecraft.  A minimum impulse bit of 0.04 N.s (0.01 lbf.s) is achieved via a fast-actuating valve and optimized thrust chamber geometry enabling Rendezvous, Proximity Operations and Docking (RPOD). Operating at 200s of specific impulse (ISP) maximizes spacecraft maneuverability and longevity enabling it to complete more RPOD operations when compared with less performant thrusters.

M4 employs a highly optimized design fully utilizing the latest additive manufacturing techniques resulting in an extremely lightweight propulsion system, freeing up mass for other subsystems such as payload, power, or fuel.  The thruster is delivered ready for spacecraft integration with convenient flying leads for the electric catalyst and valve heaters and thermistors, and pre-installed fluid interfaces all allowing for faster vehicle integration.   

Overall, M4 provides an extremely mass efficient and low-cost propulsion solution for small spacecraft.

### A110 Bipropellant Thruster

#### Summary 

A110 is a 111 N (25 lbf), M20 bipropellant, radiatively-cooled thruster for spacecraft attitude control and ŒîV maneuvers. The thruster includes two direct-acting, single-seat solenoid valves for fast startup and shutdown responses, a refractory-metal chamber and nozzle, and a low-dribble volume multi-element injector. The additively manufactured injector enables precision pulses of less than 5 milliseconds and high-performance steady-state operation.

A110 has undergone an extensive hotfire test campaign in the flight configuration, with a total on-time of 1200s+ seconds across a variety of test conditions.

### LE144 Bipropellant Thruster

#### Summary

The LE144 is a 144 N (32 lbf) Hydrazine/MON3 thruster. The LE144 achieves unprecedented performance and a wide operating envelope by using a novel micro-coaxial gas-gas injection scheme and an innovative regeneratively cooled combustion chamber geometry.

The LE144 can run in monopropellant mode, allowing total depletion of spacecraft propellant.

In hotfire testing the thruster has achieved 15,455 seconds of accumulated on time and an impressive maximum continuous burn time of 6,000 seconds over mixture ratios ranging from 0.8 to 1.6.

## A2200 Bipropellant Engine

#### Summary

A2200 is a 500-lbf bipropellant hypergolic engine developed by Agile to provide maximum performance on demanding missions, with a specific impulse of more than 318 seconds.

Using an integral pintle sleeve throttling mechanism, the engine is capable of deep throttling, providing a smooth ride and fine control for a variety of missions. The engine can throttle from 50 to 100% thrust in under 650 milliseconds.

The MON3 and M20 propellants are provided to half-inch inlets at pressures between 470 and 530 psia. The A2200 engine design extensively leverages additive manufacturing resulting in an engine that is only 5.9 kilograms. The engine is qualified for 1000 seconds of firing time after delivery and 30 starts.
